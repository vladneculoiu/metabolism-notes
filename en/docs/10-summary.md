# Summary

> The complete map + the 5 ideas to remember.

---

## The complete metabolism map

### STORAGE mode: high insulin, after meals

```text
CARBOHYDRATES:
Glucose -> priority: energy now (brain, organs)
           -> glycogenesis -> liver + muscle glycogen
           -> lipogenesis (DNL) -> FAT (only final surplus)

FATS:
Fatty acids -> esterification -> triglycerides -> ADIPOCYTES
   (LPL active, HSL blocked by insulin)

PROTEIN:
   Amino acids -> protein synthesis -> MUSCLE / organs / enzymes
              -> surplus: deamination -> urea + energy/fat (rare)
```

### BURNING mode: low insulin, fasting/effort/deficit

```text
GLYCOGEN:
   Glycogenolysis -> glucose -> glycolysis -> ATP + CO2 + water

FAT:
   Lipolysis (HSL active) -> fatty acids -> beta-oxidation -> ATP + CO2 + water

MUSCLE, to avoid:
   Muscle proteolysis -> amino acids -> gluconeogenesis -> ATP
    Appears with: intense effort + empty glycogen + insufficient protein
```

### EMERGENCY mode: empty glycogen + prolonged fast

```text
Gluconeogenesis: protein from diet or muscle + glycerol -> new glucose
Ketogenesis: fatty acids -> ketone bodies, alternative brain fuel
```

## Possible conversions

```text
Glucose  -> Fat            (lipogenesis)
Protein  -> Glucose        (gluconeogenesis)
Protein  -> Fat            (only large excess, rare)
Fat      -> Ketones        (ketogenesis)
 Fat      -> Glucose        (ONLY glycerol, tiny amount)
Fat      -> Glycogen       (cannot happen; biochemical limit)
Glucose  -> Protein        (essential amino acids are missing)
```

## Final comparison table

| | Carbohydrates | Fats | Protein |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Break down into | Glucose | Fatty acids | Amino acids |
| Raise insulin | A lot | Almost not at all | Moderately |
| Buffer storage | Glycogen (~500g) | No, direct fat storage | No, ~100g pool |
| Fuel speed | Fast | Slow | Slow, backup |
| Best for | Intense effort | Rest, light effort | Muscle building |
| Stored as | Glycogen -> fat | Fat | Muscle, no reservoir |
| Energy | 4 kcal/g | 9 kcal/g | 4 kcal/g |
| Thermic effect | ~5-10% | ~2-3% | ~25-30% |
| Satiety | Medium | Good | **Highest** |

## Metabolic processes in one table

| Process | What it does | Insulin |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Glycolysis** | Glucose -> ATP, immediate burning | -- |
| **Glycogenesis** | Glucose -> glycogen, storage | High |
| **Glycogenolysis** | Glycogen -> glucose, release | Low |
| **Lipogenesis** | Glucose -> fat, storage | High |
| **Lipolysis** | Fat -> fatty acids, release | Low |
| **Beta-oxidation** | Fatty acids -> ATP, burning | Low |
| **Gluconeogenesis** | Protein/fat components -> glucose | Low |
| **Protein synthesis** | Amino acids -> muscle, building | Moderate |
| **Muscle proteolysis** | Muscle -> amino acids, catabolism | -- |
| **Ketogenesis** | Fat -> ketone bodies | Low |
| **Esterification** | Fatty acids -> triglycerides, storage in adipocytes | High |

## Factors that block fat burning

**In order of impact:**

1. **Alcohol**: highest burning priority, stops fat burning
2. **Poor sleep**: the whole mechanism worsens: cortisol, ghrelin, leptin, insulin
3. **Chronic stress**: cortisol -> blood glucose -> insulin, even without eating
4. **Chronically high insulin**: sugar all day, no burning window
5. **Sedentary lifestyle**: insulin-resistant muscles, glycogen always full
6. **Frequent snacks**: insulin never drops
7. **Insulin resistance**: permanent lock on fat

## The 5 core ideas

### 1. Insulin is the switch

```text
High = storage (LPL active, HSL blocked)
Low  = burning (HSL active, lipolysis on)
```

Control insulin through what and when you eat, and you influence body composition.

---

### 2. A calorie deficit is rule #1

```text
Low insulin = the door is open for burning
Calorie deficit = the reason the body walks through it
```

You need both. Low insulin without a deficit does not cause fat loss. A deficit without low-insulin windows still works, but feels harder.

---

### 3. Protein is the priority in a deficit

- It is not stored: small pool, distribute through the day
- It almost never turns into fat
- It protects and builds muscle
- It is the most satiating
- It has the highest thermic effect

```text
Target: 2-2.2g/kg body weight/day, split across 3-4 meals
```

---

### 4. You do not choose where fat comes off

- Genetics and hormones decide distribution
- Belly fat leaves last, subcutaneously, but dangerous visceral fat leaves first
- Local exercises do not burn fat from the trained area: spot reduction is a myth
- Solution: deficit + time + sleep + lower stress

---

### 5. Movement and quantity are the two levers

```text
Movement -> empties glycogen -> carbs go to muscle, not fat
                            -> increases the daily calorie buffer
                            -> improves insulin sensitivity

Quantity, the deficit -> guarantees that net burning exceeds storage
```

Without movement, glycogen is always full and surplus becomes fat. Without a deficit, even low insulin does not make fat disappear.

---

## The recomposition formula

```text
SMALL deficit (-200-300 kcal)
   + HIGH protein (2-2.2g/kg), distributed
   + WEIGHT training with progression, 3-4x/week
   + Good SLEEP, 7-9h
   ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------
   = Fat loss + muscle gain at the same time
```

> Simple and consistent beats complex and perfect. 3-4 months of correct application can create a visible, real transformation.
