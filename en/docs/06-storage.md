# Storage

> Liver, muscle, and adipocytes: how each works and what they decide about your body fat.

---

## The two glycogen stores

Glycogen is stored in **two completely separate places**, with different roles:

### Liver glycogen (~100g)

```text
Role: RESERVOIR FOR THE WHOLE BODY

Liver -> glycogenolysis -> glucose -> BLOOD -> whole body
   (brain, organs, inactive muscles)

It empties:
   -> overnight (8h without food = liver nearly empty in the morning)
   -> between meals
   -> during any period without carbohydrates
```

### Muscle glycogen (~300-400g)

```text
Role: EXCLUSIVE FUEL FOR THAT SPECIFIC MUSCLE

Muscle -> glycogenolysis -> glucose -> burned ON SITE
   (does NOT enter the blood, does NOT help other organs/muscles)

It empties:
   -> ONLY during physical effort by that specific muscle
   -> it stays intact if you do not train
```

> **The "selfish" rule:** muscle lacks the enzyme glucose-6-phosphatase, which would allow glucose to be sent into the blood. That is why muscles do not regulate blood glucose. The liver is the one that can.

---

## Direct comparison

| | Liver glycogen | Muscle glycogen |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Capacity | ~100g, small | ~300-400g, large |
| For whom | **Whole body** | Only its own muscle |
| Sends glucose into blood | **Yes** | **No** |
| Empties when | Overnight, between meals | During physical effort |
| Refills when | At any meal with carbs | Only if effort depleted it |

---

## How both stores fill

**Important:** they fill **simultaneously and independently**, not in sequence:

```text
Glucose in blood after a meal
        |
        +--> LIVER
        |
        +--> MUSCLE
             stores fill in parallel depending on how empty they are

Both full?
        |
        v
YES -> surplus -> FAT (lipogenesis)
```

> The "liver first, then muscles" myth is wrong. Each store pulls glucose independently according to its state. Muscles emptied by effort pull more aggressively because exercise activates GLUT4 transporters. Only after **both** are full does surplus become fat.

---

## Storage state in the morning

In the morning you are **not** completely empty:

| Store | State on waking |
| --- | --- |
| Liver glycogen | Partly empty, about 60-70% used during sleep |
| Muscle glycogen | Almost full, because muscles do not work during sleep |

> **Implication:** mornings are a good burning window: low insulin, partly empty liver. But you still have energy for activity and training from intact muscle glycogen.

## Adipocytes: the fat store

### What they are

```text
Adipocyte = balloon-like cell specialized in triglyceride storage
   -> fat occupies ~90% of the cell
   -> the nucleus is pushed to the side
```

### Dynamics

```text
You gain fat -> adipocytes SWELL
You lose fat -> adipocytes EMPTY and SHRINK
               (the number of cells does NOT decrease)
```

> This is why regaining fat is easy: the empty cells remain there, ready to refill. Fat loss empties them; it does not eliminate them.

### Number of adipocytes

```text
In adults, the number is relatively FIXED (~10-30 billion)
   -> small fat gain = existing cells swell
   -> large fat gain, once cells max out = body creates NEW CELLS
   -> new cells do not disappear with weight loss
```

### Adipocytes are not passive bags

They are an **active hormonal organ** and influence metabolism through hormones:

| Hormone/substance | Effect |
| --- | --- |
| **Leptin** | Signals "I am full" and regulates hunger |
| **Adiponectin** | Improves insulin sensitivity |
| **Inflammatory substances** | Secreted by visceral fat -> insulin resistance |

> Visceral belly fat is dangerous partly because those adipocytes release inflammatory substances that disrupt metabolism.

---

## Types of adipocytes

| Type | Color | Role |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **White fat** | White/yellowish | Stores energy, most of your body fat |
| **Brown fat** | Brownish | **Burns** energy to produce heat |
| **Beige fat** | Intermediate | White fat that can become more "burning" with cold/exercise |

---

## Where fat is stored: you do not choose

Distribution is decided by **genetics + hormones**, not local exercises:

| Factor | Effect |
| --- | --- |
| **Genetics** | The baseline pattern inherited from family |
| **Testosterone** in men | Favors **abdominal** storage, the "apple" shape |
| **Estrogen** in women | Favors **hips/thighs** storage, the "pear" shape |
| **Cortisol** from stress | Favors dangerous **visceral** abdominal fat |

### Menopause

Estrogen falls, so women often begin storing more fat around the abdomen, similar to men. This confirms the hormone's direct role.

---

## The order fat leaves during weight loss

```text
1. VISCERAL fat around organs      -> leaves FIRST  (good for health, less visible)
2. Face, neck, arms                -> visible quickly
3. Chest, back
4. Thighs, glutes
5. Abdominal SUBCUTANEOUS fat      -> leaves LAST  (the visible belly fat)
```

> **Why belly fat is last:** subcutaneous abdominal adipocytes have more alpha receptors, which slow lipolysis, and fewer beta receptors, which start it. The brake is pulled harder there.

### The spot reduction myth

```text
Crunches -> do NOT burn belly fat
Squats   -> do NOT burn thigh fat
Exercise burns fat from the WHOLE body, in the order genetics dictates
```
