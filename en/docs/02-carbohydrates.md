# Carbohydrates

> From fork to ATP: the full path of glucose through the body.

---

## The full path

```text
1. YOU EAT (bread, rice, fruit, sugar)
        
2. DIGESTION -> carbohydrates break down into GLUCOSE
        
3. Glucose enters the blood -> blood glucose rises
        
4. The pancreas senses it -> releases INSULIN
        
5. Insulin directs glucose, in priority order:
        ---- ENERGY NOW (brain, organs, muscles)  priority #1
        ---- GLYCOGENESIS -> liver + muscle glycogen
        ---- LIPOGENESIS -> fat (only the final surplus)
        
6. BURNING (glycolysis):
Glucose -> mitochondria -> ATP
   Final products: CO2 + water
```

---

## Priority order matters

Glucose does **not** go straight to fat storage. The real order is:

| Priority | Destination | Who uses it |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **#1** | Immediate energy | Brain (~120g/day), organs, cell maintenance -- nonstop |
| **#2** | Glycogen refill | Liver + muscle, if there is room (~500g total) |
| **#3** | Fat | Only the final surplus, after #1 and #2 are covered |

> The daily "buffer" for a sedentary person is around ~200g of carbohydrates: energy + liver refill that can be used without becoming fat. Physical activity greatly increases this buffer.

---

## Blood glucose and insulin: the thermostat

```text
PANCREAS = sensor (thermostat)
   -> senses blood glucose -> sends commands through hormones

LIVER = executor
   -> receives commands -> stores or releases glucose
```

| Situation | Pancreas | Liver |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Blood glucose rises after a meal | Releases **insulin** | Stores glucose as glycogen |
| Blood glucose falls between meals | Releases **glucagon** | Releases glucose from glycogen |

## Refined sugar: what it is and why it matters

Industrially processed sugar, stripped of fiber, vitamins, and minerals. No fiber -> instant absorption -> sharp insulin spike.

### The key difference: fiber

```text
Whole apple  -> fiber slows absorption -> insulin rises slowly
Apple juice  -> no fiber -> insulin rises sharply (= liquid sugar)
```

### Simple recognition test

> *"Could I eat this in its natural form?"*
>
> - Raw sugar cane -> yes, it contains lots of fiber
> - A teaspoon of white sugar -> instant, pure -> refined

### Common forms to avoid

**Obvious:** white/brown sugar, processed honey, glucose-fructose syrup, high-fructose corn syrup (HFCS), agave syrup.

**Hidden in products:** juices, energy drinks, breakfast cereal, "protein" bars, fruit yogurts, ketchup, sweet sauces, white bread, pastries.

**Label trap:** sugar hides under dozens of names:
> glucose syrup, dextrose, maltose, sucrose, evaporated cane juice, maltodextrin, fruit juice concentrate...

### What is not refined sugar

- Whole fruit, with its fiber
- Complex carbohydrates: oats, brown rice, sweet potato, legumes
- Vegetables

## Glycogen: the storage system

| | Liver glycogen | Muscle glycogen |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Capacity | ~100g | ~300-400g |
| For whom | **Whole body** | Only that specific muscle |
| Can send glucose into blood | **Yes** | **No** |
| Empties | Overnight, between meals | Only during effort |
| Refills | At any meal with carbs | Only after effort that used it |

> **The two stores fill simultaneously and independently**, not in sequence ("first liver, then muscles"). Each one pulls glucose according to how empty it is. Only after **both** are full does the surplus become fat.

### How long it takes to empty

| Situation | Time |
| --- | --- |
| Complete rest, no food | 24-36h |
| Sleep (8h) | Empties **liver** glycogen because the liver maintains blood glucose |
| Intense training (sprints, football) | 1-2h can empty much of **muscle** glycogen |

### How long it takes to refill after effort

```text
Carbs after sport  -> full refill in ~24h
No carbs           -> slow, partial refill from protein (~48-72h)
```

## Snacks and the burning window

Every snack **restarts Phase 1**: high insulin, interrupted fat burning.

```text
8:00  breakfast -> insulin high
10:30 snack     -> insulin high AGAIN (no time to burn)
13:00 lunch     -> insulin high
16:00 snack     -> insulin high AGAIN
-> the body never enters burning mode
```

**Ideal for recomposition:** 2-3 clear meals, no snacks -> 4-5h windows where insulin falls and fat burning can happen.

**If you need a snack:** protein + fat, such as eggs, nuts, or cheese. They do not raise insulin aggressively.

---

## Particularities

- The only macro with a dedicated **buffer store**: glycogen
- Raise insulin **the most** of all macronutrients
- **Fast** fuel for intense/explosive effort: sprints, lifting
- **Refined** = sharp spike; **complex** = slower, steadier absorption
- Energy: **4 kcal/g**
