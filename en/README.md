# Macronutrient Metabolism

> A practical illustrated guide to how **carbohydrates, fats, and protein** are burned and stored, and how to apply that to body recomposition: losing fat while gaining muscle.

---

## About

This is a personal reference guide to macronutrient metabolism, written in accessible but rigorous language. It starts from simple questions like "what happens when I eat?" and builds toward concrete mechanisms: insulin, glycogen, lipolysis, and recomposition.

**Who it is for:** anyone who wants to understand *why* a diet works, or fails, beyond memorized rules.

## The 5 Core Ideas

1. **Insulin is the switch.** High = storage, low = burning.
2. **A calorie deficit is rule #1.** Insulin opens the door; the deficit makes the body walk through it.
3. **Protein is the priority**, especially in a deficit: it is not stored, it practically does not make you fat, and it protects muscle.
4. **You do not choose where fat comes off.** Genetics and hormones decide; belly fat leaves last.
5. **Movement + quantity** are the two real levers.
