# Glossary

> All technical terms from the guide, alphabetically.

---

## A

**Adipocytes** -- cells specialized in storing fat as triglycerides. They swell during fat gain and empty during fat loss, but their number remains relatively fixed in adults.

**Adiponectin** -- hormone secreted by adipocytes that improves insulin sensitivity.

**Alpha receptor** -- receptor on adipocytes that **slows** lipolysis; more common in the abdominal area, which helps explain stubborn belly fat.

**Amino acids** -- the "bricks" proteins are built from. There are 20 types, 9 of which are essential and must come from the diet.

**Amino acid pool** -- the sum of free amino acids in blood + intracellular fluid; small (~100g), emptied within a few hours, with no dedicated reserve.

**ATP** (adenosine triphosphate) -- the universal energy "currency" of the cell, produced by burning glucose, fatty acids, or amino acids.

---

## B

**Beta-oxidation** -- the process by which fatty acids are burned in mitochondria to produce ATP; final products are CO2 and water.

**Beta receptor** -- receptor on adipocytes that **starts** lipolysis; more common in easier-to-lean areas such as face and arms.

**Blood glucose** -- the level of glucose in the blood, measured in mg/dL or mmol/L. It is a measurement, not a separate substance.

---

## C

**Calorie deficit** -- a state in which you consume less energy than you burn; the necessary condition for fat loss.

**Catabolism** -- breakdown of body structures such as muscle, glycogen, or fat for energy; the opposite of anabolism.

**Chylomicrons** -- transport "vehicles" that carry fat from the intestine; they bypass the liver at first, enter lymph, then blood.

**Cortisol** -- stress hormone from the adrenal glands. Chronically high cortisol favors visceral fat, insulin resistance, and muscle catabolism.

**Creatine phosphate / phosphocreatine** -- stored form of creatine in muscle; rapidly regenerates ATP for short bursts such as sprints and lifts.

---

## D

**Deamination** -- removal of the amino group, nitrogen, from an amino acid. Nitrogen becomes urea, eliminated in urine; the rest becomes energy or glucose.

---

## E

**Esterification** -- reassembly of fatty acids into triglycerides for storage in adipocytes.

---

## F

**Fatty acids** -- forms in which fat circulates through blood after digestion; once inside mitochondria, they produce ATP through beta-oxidation.

**Fructose** -- simple sugar, half of table sugar, processed almost exclusively by the liver. In excess it drives liver lipogenesis and fatty liver.

---

## G

**Glucagon** -- pancreatic hormone that opposes insulin; released when blood glucose falls and stimulates the liver to release glucose.

**Gluconeogenesis** -- creation of new glucose from non-carbohydrate sources such as amino acids, glycerol, and lactate; appears between meals, during intense effort with empty glycogen, or during fasting.

**Glucose** -- the main cellular fuel; comes from carbohydrates and from gluconeogenesis.

**Glucose-6-phosphatase** -- enzyme that allows glucose to be released into blood. Present in the **liver**, absent in muscle.

**GLUT4** -- glucose transporter in muscle cells; activated by insulin **and** by muscle contraction, which is why movement improves insulin sensitivity.

**Glycogen** -- storage form of glucose, stored in liver (~100g) and muscle (~300-400g); broken down quickly when energy is needed.

**Glycogenesis** -- formation of glycogen from glucose; activated by insulin.

**Glycogenolysis** -- breakdown of glycogen into glucose; activated by glucagon/adrenaline when blood glucose falls.

**Glycolysis** -- burning glucose to produce ATP; can occur without oxygen during intense effort or with oxygen during aerobic metabolism.

---

## H

**HbA1c** -- blood marker that estimates average blood glucose over roughly 3 months.

**HFCS** (High-Fructose Corn Syrup) -- corn syrup rich in fructose, common in industrial soft drinks and especially hard on the liver.

**HOMA-IR** -- index calculated from fasting glucose + fasting insulin; one of the best simple markers for insulin resistance.

**HSL** (hormone-sensitive lipase) -- enzyme that releases fat from adipocytes during lipolysis; blocked by insulin and activated by glucagon/adrenaline.

---

## I

**Insulin** -- pancreatic hormone released when blood glucose rises. It activates storage through LPL, blocks fat release through HSL, and directs glucose into cells.

**Insulin resistance** -- state in which cells no longer respond normally to insulin. The pancreas compensates by producing more, causing chronically high insulin.

---

## K

**Ketogenesis** -- production of ketone bodies from fatty acids; appears during prolonged fasting or ketogenic diets and provides alternative brain fuel.

**Keto / ketogenic diet** -- very low-carbohydrate diet, often <50g/day, where insulin stays low and the body relies more on ketones.

---

## L

**Lactate** -- product of anaerobic glycolysis during intense effort; can be reconverted into glucose by the liver through the Cori cycle or oxidized directly for energy.

**Leptin** -- hormone secreted by adipocytes in proportion to fat mass; signals satiety to the brain. In obesity, leptin resistance can appear.

**Lipogenesis** (de novo, DNL) -- conversion of surplus glucose/fructose into triglycerides, mainly in the liver.

**Lipolysis** -- breakdown of triglycerides into fatty acids + glycerol; activated when insulin is low and HSL is free.

**LPL** (lipoprotein lipase) -- enzyme on adipocyte surfaces that breaks blood triglycerides apart so fatty acids can enter the cell for storage; activated by insulin.

---

## M

**Mitochondrion** -- the cell's "power plant", where ATP is produced by oxidizing glucose or fatty acids.

**Muscle proteolysis** -- breakdown of muscle protein into amino acids; catabolism to avoid unless medically necessary.

---

## N

**NAFLD** (Non-Alcoholic Fatty Liver Disease) -- fatty liver without alcohol; caused by fructose, chronic calorie surplus, and insulin resistance.

---

## P

**Pancreas** -- gland with digestive and hormonal roles; beta cells produce insulin, alpha cells produce glucagon.

**Protein synthesis** -- building new proteins from amino acids; the process through which muscle grows, stimulated by effort + protein intake.

**Proteolysis** -- breakdown of proteins into amino acids; can be digestive and normal, or muscular and catabolic.

---

## S

**Spot reduction** -- the myth that local exercises, such as crunches, burn fat from that exact area. False: fat is burned from the whole body in a genetically determined order.

**Steatosis** -- fatty liver; accumulation of triglycerides in liver cells above ~5-10%.

---

## T

**Thermic effect** -- calories burned to digest and process food: protein ~25-30%, carbohydrates ~5-10%, fats ~2-3%.

**Triglycerides** -- storage form of fat in adipocytes: 3 fatty acids + 1 glycerol; also measurable in blood.

---

## U

**Urea** -- nitrogen waste product from amino acid deamination; eliminated by kidneys in urine. High protein intake means more urea and more water needed.

---

## V

**VLDL** (Very Low-Density Lipoprotein) -- the liver's triglyceride transport "trucks" that move fat through blood toward adipocytes. High values suggest hepatic overproduction of fat.
