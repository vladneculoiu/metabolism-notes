## Essential vocabulary

| Term | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| **Glucose** | The actual fuel that comes from carbohydrates |
| **Blood glucose** | The *level* of glucose in the blood; a measurement, not a separate substance |
| **Insulin** | The key hormone that directs glucose, produced by the pancreas |
| **Glucagon** | Insulin's opposite hormone: releases glucose from storage |
| **Glycogen** | The storage form of glucose, stored in liver + muscle |
| **Adipocytes** | Cells specialized for fat storage |
| **Amino acids** | The "bricks" proteins are made from |
| **Triglycerides** | The form in which fat is stored in adipocytes |
| **ATP** | Adenosine triphosphate, the universal energy "currency" of the cell |

## The three macronutrients: main roles

| Macronutrient | Main role | Energy |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Carbohydrates** | Fast fuel | 4 kcal/g |
| **Fats** | Slow fuel + hormonal structure | 9 kcal/g |
| **Protein** | Building and repair: muscle, organs, enzymes | 4 kcal/g |

---

## Process logic: `-genesis` vs `-lysis`

Most metabolic process names follow a simple logic:

- **`-genesis`** = *making* something: building or storing
- **`-lysis`** = *breaking down* something: release or burning

### Fundamental processes

| Process | What it does | When it activates |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Glycogenesis** | Glucose -> glycogen (storage) | After meals, high insulin |
| **Glycogenolysis** | Glycogen -> glucose (release) | Between meals, physical effort |
| **Lipogenesis** | Glucose/surplus -> fat (storage) | Calorie surplus, full glycogen |
| **Lipolysis** | Fat -> fatty acids (release) | Low insulin, exercise, fasting |
| **Beta-oxidation** | Fatty acids -> ATP (burning) | Low insulin, need for energy |
| **Gluconeogenesis** | Protein/fat components -> new glucose | Long fast, empty glycogen |
| **Protein synthesis** | Amino acids -> muscle/organs | Protein intake + stimulus |
| **Muscle proteolysis** | Muscle -> amino acids | Low protein + need for glucose |
| **Ketogenesis** | Fat -> ketone bodies | Prolonged fasting, keto |

## The central switch: insulin

All of metabolism can be reduced to one question: **how high is insulin?**

```text
HIGH INSULIN -> STORAGE mode
   -> glucose and fat go into storage
   -> stored fat cannot leave

LOW INSULIN -> BURNING mode
   -> fat leaves storage
   -> the body burns reserves
```

> Every following chapter returns to this rule. It is the thread that ties everything together.

---

## What can turn into what

Not every conversion is possible. These are the fixed rules:

```text
Glucose  -> Fat            (lipogenesis)
Protein  -> Glucose        (gluconeogenesis)
Protein  -> Fat            (only with a large excess)
Fat      -> Ketones        (ketogenesis)
 Fat      -> Glucose        (ONLY glycerol, a tiny amount)
Fat      -> Glycogen       (cannot happen)
Glucose  -> Protein        (essential amino acids are missing)
```

> The two **NO** rules explain a lot:
>
> - Fat does not refill glycogen -> intense effort while depleted can attack muscle
> - Glucose does not build muscle -> carbohydrates do not replace protein
