# 03 -- Fats

> LPL, HSL, and why fat alone is not the enemy: the sugar + fat combination is the problem.

[Carbohydrates](02-carbohydrates.md) | [Protein](04-protein.md)

---

## The full path

```text
1. YOU EAT (oil, butter, eggs, nuts, avocado, meat)
        
2. DIGESTION -> FATTY ACIDS + glycerol
        
3. Reassembled into TRIGLYCERIDES -> packed into chylomicrons
   -> enter LYMPH -> then BLOOD
   (they initially bypass the liver)
        
4. They do NOT raise blood glucose -> insulin barely moves
        
5. At adipocytes: LPL breaks triglycerides apart
        
6. Two paths, depending on insulin:
        ---- LOW insulin -> fatty acids -> direct burning
        ---- HIGH insulin -> ESTERIFICATION -> storage in adipocytes
        
7. BURNING (lipolysis -> beta-oxidation):
   Triglycerides -> fatty acids -> mitochondria -> ATP
   Final products: CO2 + water (you exhale part of the fat)
```

---

## The two key enzymes

This is the real mechanism behind fat storage vs fat burning:

| Enzyme | Role | Controlled by insulin |
|---|---|---|
| **LPL** (lipoprotein lipase) | Puts fat **INTO** adipocytes (storage) | Insulin **activates** it |
| **HSL** (hormone-sensitive lipase) | Pulls fat **OUT OF** adipocytes (burning) | Insulin **blocks** it |

```text
HIGH insulin:
   LPL active   -> fat enters storage 
   HSL blocked  -> fat cannot leave

LOW insulin:
   LPL inactive -> fat is no longer stored as easily
   HSL active   -> fat leaves -> burning
```

> **Summary:** insulin locks adipocytes. While insulin is high, fat is trapped inside, even if you are in a calorie deficit. At that specific moment, you are not burning stored fat.

---

## The sugar + fat trap
Fat alone is not stored aggressively. It becomes a problem in combination:

| Food | What happens |
|---|---|
| Nuts (mostly fat) | Insulin does not rise much -> burned or "ignored" |
| Chocolate (fat + sugar) | Sugar raises insulin -> fat is stored |
| Fries (starch + oil) | The perfect storage combination |
| Avocado + salad (fat + fiber) | Stable insulin -> burned |

> **Rule:** refined carbohydrates raise insulin -> insulin opens adipocytes -> fat from **the same meal** goes directly into storage.

---

## How stored fat is burned

```text
CONDITION: insulin must be LOW

Glucagon/adrenaline signal adipocytes
        
HSL activates -> LIPOLYSIS
        
Triglycerides -> fatty acids + glycerol
        
Fatty acids -> blood -> muscles/organs
        
BETA-OXIDATION in mitochondria -> ATP
        
Final products: CO2 + water
```

> **Surprising fact:** you literally exhale part of your fat as CO2. The rest becomes water, eliminated through urine and sweat.

---

## Stored fat: where and how
Fat is stored in **adipocytes**, balloon-like cells filled with triglycerides, in several places:

| Storage type | Where | Risk | Leaves |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Visceral** | Around organs (hard belly) | Very high | **First** when losing weight |
| **Subcutaneous** | Under skin (soft belly, thighs) | Lower | **Last** when losing weight |
| **Ectopic** | Liver, pancreas, heart | Warning sign | Early |

> **Useful paradox:** the most dangerous fat, visceral fat, leaves first when you lose weight, even if the mirror does not show it immediately.

---

## Why belly fat leaves last

Abdominal adipocytes have **more alpha receptors**, which slow lipolysis, and fewer beta receptors, which start lipolysis. In practice, the "brake" is pulled harder there.

You cannot force the area with local exercises, the "spot reduction" myth:

```text
Crunches -> do NOT burn belly fat
             -> they strengthen the muscle under it
-> Fat is burned from the whole body, in a genetically determined order
```

---

## Particularities

- Do **not** raise insulin much on their own, the main difference from carbohydrates
- Have **no buffer store**: they are burned right away or stored directly
- **Slow** fuel, ideal at rest and during light/moderate effort
- Most **calorie-dense**: 9 kcal/g, compared with 4 kcal/g for carbs and protein
- They are **not the enemy**. The problem is sugar + fat + calorie surplus.
