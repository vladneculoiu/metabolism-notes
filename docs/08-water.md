# 08 -- Water

> Water's role in burning, storage, and scale fluctuations: more important than it seems.

[Insulin Resistance](07-insulin-resistance-fatty-liver.md) | [Recomposition](09-recomposition.md)

---

## Water is a final product of burning

When you burn fat or glucose, water is one of the final products:

```text
Fat      -> beta-oxidation -> ATP + CO2 + WATER
Glucose  -> glycolysis     -> ATP + CO2 + WATER
```

> **Surprising fact:** when you lose fat, part of that fat literally becomes water, eliminated through urine, sweat, and breath, plus CO2 that you exhale.

---

## Water is required to burn fat

Lipolysis, the breakdown of fat, is a **hydrolysis** reaction. Hydro = water.

```text
Triglycerides + WATER -> fatty acids + glycerol
                    ^
              no water -> the reaction is less efficient
```

> Dehydration makes lipolysis less efficient, so fat burning is harder. Hydration **supports** burning.

---

## Water and glycogen: why the scale fluctuates

The most practical water lesson for fat loss:

```text
1g glycogen binds ~3g WATER
Total glycogen: ~500g -> binds ~1.5 L of water
```

### What happens on the scale

| Situation | Scale | Reality |
|---|---|---|
| Start low-carb/fasting | Down 1-2 kg in a few days | Glycogen + water, NOT fat |
| Eat carbs again | Up 1-2 kg | Glycogen + water, NOT fat |
| Intense match + carb meal | Up the next day | Refilled glycogen + water |
| Match without carbs after | Down more | Empty glycogen -> less water |

> **Golden rule:** daily 0.5-2 kg scale swings are **almost always water**, tied to glycogen, salt, and hydration. Real fat changes slowly: at most ~0.5 kg/week in good conditions.

## Water and protein
Protein digestion produces **urea**, a nitrogen waste product from deamination, eliminated in urine:

```text
Amino acids -> deamination -> urea + energy
                              
                       eliminated by KIDNEYS
                              
                       requires WATER for flushing
```

> With high protein intake, 2g/kg+, you need more water so kidneys can eliminate urea without unnecessary strain.

---

## Water and satiety

Simple but real mechanisms:

- **Before meals:** partly full stomach -> you eat less
- **Thirst confused with hunger:** many people eat when they are actually thirsty
- **Estimate:** ~500ml water 30 minutes before a meal may reduce calorie intake by ~13% in small studies

---

## Water and performance

```text
Dehydration of 1-2% of body weight
   -> physical performance drops 10-20%
   -> less strength, faster fatigue
   -> muscles access glycogen less efficiently

Dehydration of 3%+
   -> concentration and coordination suffer
   -> cramp risk rises
```

---

## How much to drink

| Situation | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Baseline, sedentary | ~30 ml/kg body weight/day |
| Physically active | +500-1000 ml above baseline |
| High protein, 2g/kg+ | +500 ml above baseline |
| Hot weather | +500-1000 ml above baseline |

**Example:** 80kg active person with high protein intake -> ~2.5-3.5 liters/day.

### Simple hydration test

```text
Light yellow urine       -> well hydrated
Dark yellow/orange urine -> drink more 
Completely clear urine   -> you may be overdoing it, rarely a problem
```

---

## Hydration around effort

### Before

```text
2-3 hours before -> drink freely, hydrate well
1 hour before    -> moderate, regular sips
 15-20 min before -> a little, if thirsty
A lot all at once right before -> running discomfort
```

### During effort

- Take **small frequent sips** during breaks, not half a bottle at once
- Long effort >60 min or heat -> water + **electrolytes** such as sodium and potassium

### After effort

- Rehydrate gradually
- A pinch of salt in water helps fluid retention and absorption

---

## Summary: water's roles

| Context | Role |
|---|---|
| Fat burning | Final product: fat -> CO2 + water |
| Lipolysis | Hydrolysis reaction, **requires water** |
| Glycogen | Each gram binds ~3g water -> scale swings |
| Protein | Removes urea through kidneys, requiring water |
| Satiety | Reduces hunger and helps the calorie deficit |
| Performance | Dehydration reduces strength and endurance |
