# 09 -- Body Recomposition

> Practical application: how to lose fat and gain muscle at the same time.

[Water](08-water.md) | [Summary](10-summary.md)

## What recomposition is

Body recomposition = fat loss + muscle gain **at the same time**.

It asks for conditions that seem opposite:

- **Fat loss** -> calorie deficit -> low insulin -> burn fat
- **Muscle gain** -> energy availability -> insulin/anabolism -> build muscle

```text
LARGE deficit -> faster fat loss
              -> but fewer resources for muscle building
              -> higher risk of muscle loss

SMALL deficit -> slower fat loss
              -> enough resources remain for muscle building
```

---

## Who recomposes most easily

| Factor | Advantage |
| --- | --- |
| **Beginner to lifting** | Biggest advantage, "newbie gains" |
| **Has fat to lose**, 3+ kg | Large advantage: fat provides energy for building |
| Returning after a break | Large advantage: muscle memory |
| Advanced and already lean | Small advantage: bulk/cut phases often work better |

## The 4 essential pillars

### 1. Small calorie deficit

```text
Pure fat loss:      large deficit (-500-700 kcal)
RECOMPOSITION:      small deficit (-200-300 kcal)
Muscle gain phase:  surplus       (+200-300 kcal)
```

A small deficit is enough to lose fat while leaving resources for muscle building.

### 2. Protein: 2-2.2g/kg body weight/day

Absolute priority. **Distributed** across the day, not eaten in one meal:

```text
80kg body weight -> 160-176g protein/day
   Example: 4 meals x 40-44g protein
```

Protein:

- Protects muscle from catabolism in a deficit
- Builds new muscle through protein synthesis
- Keeps you the fullest
- Is almost never stored as fat

### 3. Weight training: 3-4x/week

**The most important stimulus.** Without it, protein does not build muscle because the "command" is missing.

- Compound exercises: squats, bench press, pull-ups, deadlifts
- **Progression**: increasing weights/reps -> continuous growth stimulus
- Moderate cardio, not excessive, because too much can raise cortisol

### 4. Sleep: 7-9 hours

Muscle building and recovery happen **during sleep**, not in the gym.

- ~60-70% of testosterone is produced at night
- One week of poor sleep can reduce testosterone by ~10-15%
- Lack of sleep raises cortisol -> belly storage + muscle breakdown

## Fuel by effort type

| Situation | What burns | Muscle |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Rest / light effort | Fat ~60-70% | Protected |
| Intense effort + full glycogen | Glycogen, explosive | Protected |
| Intense effort + empty glycogen | Some fat + **MUSCLE** | Attacked |

> **Important paradox:** rest and light effort burn more fat and protect muscle better than intense fasted effort.

---

## Strategy around training

### Before intense effort

```text
45-60 min before:
   Fast carbohydrate (banana/dates/fruit) -> ~25-50g carbs
   -> refills muscle glycogen in time
   -> muscles have fuel -> they do not attack themselves

Not necessary for:
   - light/moderate effort: walking, pilates, easy cardio
   - these efforts burn fat directly
```

### After intense effort

```text
In the first 1-2h, the anabolic window:
Protein: 30-40g -- PRIORITY, repairs muscle
   Carbs:   40-60g -- refill depleted glycogen

Why carbs are "forgiving" after effort:
   -> empty glycogen absorbs carbs directly into muscle
   -> insulin works in your favor, anti-catabolic
   -> almost nothing goes to fat because glycogen has room
```

### If you are not training hard the next day

You can skip post-effort carbs if fat loss is the goal and you are not training tomorrow:

```text
No carbs after moderate effort + enough protein:
   -> insulin stays low -> fat burning continues
   -> glycogen refills slowly through gluconeogenesis in ~48h
   -> muscle is protected by dietary protein
```

---

## How this connects to insulin

```text
TRAINING DAYS:
   Carbs around effort -> insulin high -> glycogen -> muscle

REST DAYS:
   Moderate/low carbs -> insulin low -> fat burning
   Consistent protein -> muscle protected
```

## Creatine, optional supplement

The best documented supplement for strength and muscle mass. **Not mandatory**: the basics create 90% of the result.

### What it does

```text
Creatine -> stored in muscle as PHOSPHOCREATINE
   -> rapidly regenerates ATP for short bursts: sprints, lifts
   -> more available energy -> harder training -> stronger stimulus
```

### Proven benefits

- Increased strength and power
- More muscle mass because it enables harder training
- Faster recovery
- Better strength retention in a deficit, protecting muscle

### How to take it

```text
3-5g creatine MONOHYDRATE/day, consistently
   -> including rest days
   -> no loading phase required
   -> after ~3-4 weeks, muscle stores are full
```

> **Scale note:** +1-2kg in the first weeks is intramuscular water, not fat. Hydrated muscle is more functional.

### Correct form

```text
Creatine MONOHYDRATE -> most studied, cheaper
Non-monohydrate or "fancy" forms (HCl, buffered, etc.) -> more expensive, no proven advantage
```

## Realistic expectations

```text
Recomposition is SLOW, slower than simple fat loss
   -> the scale may move little or not at all
      (fat lost + muscle gained = cancel each other on the scale)
   -> but the mirror, waist, and strength change visibly
```

### Indicators to track, not just scale weight

| Indicator | What it shows |
| --- | --- |
| **Waist circumference** | Visceral/abdominal fat loss |
| **Mirror** | Tone and shape |
| **Training weights** | Muscle gain |
| **Clothes** | The most practical measure |
| **Monthly photos** | Objective visual progress |

### Realistic timeline

```text
1 month:   training improves, more strength
2 months:  clothes feel different, waist decreases
3-4 months: -3-4kg fat + 1-2kg muscle = clear visual transformation
```
